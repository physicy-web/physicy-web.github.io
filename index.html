Dynamics

Dynamics is the branch of physics that studies how forces affect motion. It emphasizes how forces cause changes in an object’s velocity or direction based on Newton's laws. The core formula is:

F = m * a

  • F = force (N)
  • m = mass (kg)
  • a = acceleration (m/s²)

What is force?

Force in physics is any interaction that can cause a mass object to experience a change in motion, either in direction or geometric construction.


Types of forces that will be discussed in this material :

- Friction Force

- Normal Force

- External Force

- Gravitational Force

- Rope Tension Force

Friction Force

In general, every moving object will experience friction. Friction can arise because two surfaces of objects touch each other. From this contact, movement occurs in two different directions, causing an incident.


Frictional force is divided into two types, namely static friction force and kinetic friction force.


Static Friction Force

Static friction is the friction force that acts on an object that is initially still until it is about to move. As long as the pushing/pulling force of the object is less than its static friction force, the object will remain still and not move.

Kinetic Friction Force

Kinetic friction is the friction force that acts on an object that is moving, and its direction is always opposite to the direction of the object's motion.

Newton Laws

Newton's laws of motion are laws of physics that describe the displacement of an object as a result of the relationship between the magnitude and distance of the forces acting on the object.


Newton's law has 3 laws consisting of :

- Newton's First Law

- Newton's Second Law

- Newton's Third Law

Secondary Newton Laws

The formula for Newton's Second Law is:
F = m.a


Description:
F is the force (N)
m is the mass of the object (kg)
a is the acceleration (m/s2).

Third Newton Laws

His third law states that for every action (force) in nature there is an equal and opposite reaction. If object A exerts a force on object B, object B also exerts an equal and opposite force on object A. In other words, forces result from interactions. from there Newton's Third Law can be written with the equation Faction = - Freaction

There are three types of Newton's 3rd Law formula:


1. Friction force formula : Fg = u x N
Description :
Fg = friction force (N)
u = coefficient of friction
N = Normal force (N).


2. Weight force formula : w = m x g
Description :
w = weight force (N)
m = mass of an object (kg)
g = Gravity (m/s2).


3. Spesific gravity formula : s = p x g
Description :
s = spesific gravity (N/m3)
p = density (kg/m3)
g = weight of object (N).


Description:

Physics Questions - Newton's 1st Law

A block with a mass of 5 kg (weight w = 50 N) is hung by a rope tied to the roof. If the block is at rest, what is the tension in the rope?

Known: w = 50 N

Asked: T?


Your Answer: N

Correct Answer : 50N

Discussion :
ΣF = 0
T – w = 0
T – 50 = 0
T = 50 N

Physics Questions - Newton's 2st Law

A ball with a mass of 450 grams is pushed with a force of 2 N. As a result, the object that was originally still becomes moving. The acceleration of the object's motion is

m = 450 grams = 0.45 kilograms, F = 2N

Asked: a?

If your answer is a decimal, round to two decimal places

Your Answer: m/s2

Correct Answer : 4.44 m/s2

Discussion :
F = m.a
2 = 0.45
2 / 0.45 = a
4.44 = a
a = 4.44 m/s2

Physics Questions - Newton's 3st Law

A rocket with a mass of 1000 kg is launched into space. When the rocket releases gas from its engine at a speed of 500 m/s, the force generated is 5000 N. What is the reaction force generated by the rocket?

Faction = 5000N, m = 1000 kg

Asked: Freaction?

If your answer is a decimal, round to two decimal places

Your Answer: N

Correct Answer : -5000 N


Discussion :
First, you need to calculate the acceleration of the rocket produced by the gas released from its engine using Newton's Second Law.
F = m ✕ a
5000 N = 1000 kg ✕ a
a = 5 m/s2

Next, you can calculate the reaction force generated by the rocket using Newton's 3rd Law:
Faction = - Freaction
Faction = force generated by the gas from the rocket engine = 5000 N
Freaction = the reaction force generated by the rocket
Freaction = m ✕ a
Freaction = 1000 ✕ -5 Because the direction of the reaction force is opposite to the direction of the rocket's motion
Freaction = -5000N

Kinematics

Kinematics focuses on the description of motion without regard to the forces causing it. Basic kinematic equations include:

s = v₀ * t + 0.5 * a * t²

Where:

  • s = displacement (m)
  • v₀ = initial velocity (m/s)
  • t = time (s)
  • a = acceleration (m/s²)

Momentum

Momentum measures the quantity of motion in an object, a function of its mass and velocity. Momentum remains conserved in a closed system. The formula is:

p = m * v

Where:

  • p = momentum (kg m/s)
  • m = mass (kg)
  • v = velocity (m/s)
